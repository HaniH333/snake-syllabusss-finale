About This Resource

What Is Snake Syllabusss?

Snake Syllabusss is an archive-based educational resource documenting 56 species of wild snakes native to North America. It is designed to help curious people — hikers, naturalists, students, and the generally snake-curious — identify, understand, and appreciate the snakes they may encounter.

01 Purpose

Why This Site Exists

Snakes are among the most misunderstood animals in North America. Myths, fear, and misidentification lead to thousands of needless snake killings each year — and to preventable bites when people misjudge a species as harmless.

This resource exists to close that knowledge gap. Every species entry includes size, activity pattern, danger level, location data, and a written description — enough context to make an informed identification in the field.

02 Scope

What's Covered

The archive covers 56 species across five taxonomic families found in the United States, Canada and northern Mexico:

  • Colubridae — 40 species including watersnakes, gartersnakes, ratsnakes, kingsnakes, racers and others
  • Viperidae — 10 species including all major rattlesnakes, the Cottonmouth and Copperhead
  • Elapidae — 3 coral snake species
  • Boidae — 2 native boa species
  • Leptotyphlopidae — 1 threadsnake species

Each entry is a real species — no filler content. Species data is sourced from iNaturalist, the Reptile Database and peer-reviewed field guides.

03 Safety

A Note on Snake Safety

This resource is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional advice in an emergency. If you are bitten by a snake — venomous or not — seek medical attention immediately. Do not attempt to catch or kill the snake; a photo for identification is sufficient.

⚠️

In North America, call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 or go to your nearest emergency room if bitten by a suspected venomous snake.
