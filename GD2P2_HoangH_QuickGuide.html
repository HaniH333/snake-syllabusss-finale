01 ID Rules

Can't Tell if It's Venomous?

North America has many harmless snakes that mimic venomous ones. Out of the 150+ species, only 30 of them are actually venomous. A good rule of thumb is to always try to stay aware of your surroundings and where you’re stepping, especially when you're in areas you're more likely to have an encounter. If you do come into close contact with a snake, step away slowly and never handle a wild snake unless trained to do so.

🔴

📐

02 Safety

If You Encounter a Snake

Most snakebites happen when people try to handle or kill a snake. The safest rule is always the same observe from a distance and leave it alone.

👀
Keep your distance Stay at least 6 feet away. Most snakes can strike up to half their body length
ALWAYS
🚫
Never handle a wild snake Even harmless species bite when threatened, hands-off is always the right call
ALWAYS
📸
Photo, not capture A photo is enough for identification, use the zoom on your phone
TIP
🏥
If bitten — call immediately Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 · Do not cut, suck or tourniquet
EMERGENCY
⚠️

In North America, call Poison Control at 1-800-222-1222 or go to your nearest emergency room if bitten by a suspected venomous snake.
